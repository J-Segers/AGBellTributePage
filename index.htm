Alexander Graham Bell

Inventor of the first functional telephone

  • 1847

    Alexander Bell is born on March 3, 1847, in Edinburgh, Scotland.

  • 1868

    Bell gains admission to University College, London.
    But decides to end his studies to move with his family.

  • 1870

    Alexander Graham Bell moves to Ontario Canada with his family.

  • 1871

    Bell teaches at a school for the hearing impeared

  • 1873

    Alexander Graham Bell meets his wife while teaching at a school for deaf children in Boston.
    Around this time he becomes interested in communications technology and he starts working to develop technology that is capable of telegraphing harmonic sounds.

  • 1876

    Alexander Graham Bell receives a patent on technology that would transmit the human voice.
    He received the patent days before making the first succesfull test of the telephone.

  • 1880

    Bell turns his attention at to the technology of sound recording and playback.

  • 1885

    Alexander Graham Bell invents the Graphophone.

  • 1888

    Alexander Graham Bell becomes a founding member of the National Geographic Society.

  • 1898

    Bell succeeds his father in law as president.

  • 1907

    Bell founds the Aerial Experiment Association.

  • 1922

    Alexander Graham Bell passed away on August 2 on Cape Breton Island in Nova Scotia, Canada.
    During his funeral service every telephone of the bell system in the United States and Canada was kept silent in his honor.
